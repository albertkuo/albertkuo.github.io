Introduction

Hi, I'm Albert, currently a fourth year undergrad at the University of Chicago pursuing a double major in math and statistics. I'm interested in data science, machine learning, and the like.

My research experience has, for the most part, been in the field of biostatistics, where I've used both traditional statistics and machine learning models to analyze large datasets. I also hope to strengthen my skills in a wider range of fields, particularly in technology. This past year, I was a data science intern at SimpleRelevance and Rise Interactive.

In my spare time, I work on a range of projects in statistics, computing, and art. I also blog occasionally with my friend Kevin, where we talk about stats/cs things we find interesting or useful. Click on the tabs above to learn more about me or contact me at albertkuo@uchicago.edu.

Academic Projects

NLP
Natural Language Processing
I took Natural Language Processing at TTIC in winter 2016. For my final project, I used convolutional neural networks and other models to predict Kickstarter success using project pitches. Read my final paper here.
TDD
Time-Dependent Data
I took Time-Dependent Data in winter 2016. Our final project was to analyze Central England's temperatures from the mid-17th century to the present day. I used a variety of time series techniques to examine short-term and long-term cyclic patterns in the data. Read my final paper here.

Summer Projects

DIMACS
DIMACS REU 2015
I worked with Dr. Kevin Chen in the summer REU program at DIMACS (Center for Discrete Mathematics and Theoretical Computer Science). For my project, I analyzed epigenomic data and predicted enhancers using machine learning algorithms. Click here to read more about my project.
SIBS
SIBS 2014
For my final project at SIBS (Summer Institute for Training in Biostatistics), I worked on the Women's Ischemia Syndrome Evaluation (WISE) study and examined whether persistent chest pain is associated with declines in physical work capacity among women. Read my abstract here.

Side Projects

STATISTICS X
Knowvio (Statistics X)
The Statistics X app, a mobile study tool for introductory statistics, was released in April 2016. I am currently working on updating the content for the app with the Knowvio team.
METRICS
Econometrics Game

I participated in the Econometrics Game at the University of Chicago in 2015 and 2016, a day-long competition under which teams write a paper in response to a prompt and given dataset. A week later, finalists present their findings to a panel of judges.

In 2015, our prompt was to analyze the effect of obesity on healthcare costs. My team placed 2nd out of five teams in the competition and a copy of our paper can be found here.

In 2016, our prompt was to investigate the economic assimilation of immigrants to the U.S. My team placed 2nd out of nine teams in the competition and a copy of our paper can be found here.

FLAPPY BIRD
Flappy Bird
My friend Anthony and I conducted some statistical modeling and analysis of the mobile game Flappy Bird in 2014. We are currently revisiting the project and an updated version can be found here.
